Greece Advances Behind Riess Masterclass, Eliminates Baton Rouge in First Round

Today

The Greece Chicken Frogs are moving on. In a decisive Game 3 performance, Greece defeated the Baton Rouge Jumbo Shrimp 12–3 to win their first-round best-of-three series 2–1 and punch their ticket to the PJPL Summer 2025 semifinals.

Fueled by a career performance from Caje Riess, the Chicken Frogs now advance to face the top-seeded Madagascar Monkeys, who received a first-round bye.

Series Summary:

Game 1: Greece 12–0 Baton Rouge
Game 2: Baton Rouge 12–10 Greece
Game 3: Greece 12–3 Baton Rouge

What began as a lopsided shutout in Game 1 turned into a tense three-game battle — but Greece left no doubts in the deciding match.

“This was the best I’ve ever seen him play,” one PJPL analyst said of Riess. “He didn’t just win — he dictated. The serves were aggressive. The placement was clinical. It was a complete performance.”

Game-by-Game Breakdown

Game 1 set the tone. Riess came out firing, landing ace after ace and breaking down John Rusinko's rhythm early. It was the first shutout of the PJPL playoffs this season and a statement from a player often overlooked in league discussions.

“I was just in rhythm,” Riess said postgame. “Everything felt right. Serve, return, pace — it all clicked.”

But the momentum swung wildly in Game 2. The Jumbo Shrimp jumped out to a dominant 8–2 lead, with Rusinko showing rare poise and shot selection under pressure. But just as the game looked like a runaway, Greece clawed back with an aggressive 8–0 run of their own, stunning the crowd to take a 10–8 lead.

However, the comeback wasn’t enough. A pair of unforced errors from Riess and a refocused finish from Rusinko gave Baton Rouge the final four points — and a hard-earned 12–10 win. It was Rusinko’s first-ever playoff victory and a brief reminder of his grit under pressure.

Still, any optimism evaporated early in Game 3, when Riess opened with a 5–0 run and never looked back. Rusinko, visibly rattled, struggled with unforced errors and mistimed volleys as the score widened. The final margin — 12–3 — was a brutal end to what had briefly looked like a comeback.

Looking Ahead: Monkeys Await

Greece will now face the #1 seeded Madagascar Monkeys, led by league MVP frontrunner Austin Allen. The matchup pits Riess, fresh off his best form, against the league’s most dominant player — a massive challenge that will test just how far Greece’s postseason spark can carry them.

“We’re not scared,” Riess said when asked about the upcoming matchup. “They’re the number one seed, sure. But at this point in the season, we all believe we can win.”

With momentum building and confidence rising, the Chicken Frogs — soon to become the Czechia Tree Frogs — have a chance to extend their final run under the Greece banner into something truly historic.
